BJD 3D Model Physical Prototype
BJD Project

After observing the growing popularity of articulated dolls and the lack of small-scale male body types in the market, I decided to design a portable 1/12 scale BJD male body with a full range of articulation.


In recent years, 1/12 and 1/8 scale articulated dolls have become increasingly popular in the Chinese market. Among them, 1/8 scale dolls have become the mainstream choice due to their closer resemblance to standard human proportions. However, I also noticed a significant gap: the availability of small-scale articulated male bodies is very limited—especially in the portable 1/12 scale, where male character options are almost nonexistent. Nonetheless, many collectors and hobbyists continue to show interest in small, easy-to-carry male figures.

Based on this market observation, I decided to create a male 1/12 scale BJD body with a standing height of under 13 cm. I believe the 1/12 scale offers the following advantages:

• Easy to carry , ideal for outdoor photography
• Compatible with existing 1/12 scale props and accessories

I chose to start with a BJD (Ball-Jointed Doll) structure. While BJDs require several components like elastic cords, S-hooks, and aluminum wire, they are more manageable for beginners than MJDs (Mechanical Jointed Dolls), which have stricter requirements for mold accuracy and part tolerances. This made BJD a more accessible choice for my first articulated doll project.

This project aims to create a well-structured, poseable, and production-ready male body prototype as a basis for future character development, molding, and costume design.

First version: model breakdown, resin prints, and articulation testing

During testing, the first version revealed several issues: the model walls were too thin and prone to cracking, the arm joint structure limited rotation, and the shrinkage from resin printing resulted in poor fit between parts. In the second version, I addressed these problems by redesigning the arm joints and refining the part tolerances.

Second version arm joint—rounded upper section allows smoother arm rotation

In addition, I made the following improvements in the second version:
• Designed a cross-slot mechanism to prevent the elastic cords from snapping into the torso when changing hands or feet
• Added a stopper groove in the chest joint to enhance joint stability

The cross-slot is still under testing, and I am currently sourcing the correct size of elastic cord and S-hooks
